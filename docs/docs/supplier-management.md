# Supplier Management

## Overview

Supplier Management is the process of selecting, evaluating, developing, and maintaining relationships with suppliers to ensure reliable supply, quality, cost efficiency, and long-term business value.

---

## Objectives

- Build strong supplier relationships
- Improve quality
- Reduce procurement costs
- Minimize supply risks
- Ensure on-time delivery
- Drive continuous improvement

---

## Supplier Lifecycle

### 1. Identify Suppliers

Research potential suppliers that can meet business requirements.

---

### 2. Evaluate Suppliers

Assess suppliers based on:

- Quality
- Price
- Delivery
- Financial stability
- Production capacity
- Certifications

---

### 3. Supplier Selection

Choose suppliers based on the evaluation results and business needs.

---

### 4. Onboarding

Complete documentation, contracts, compliance checks, and ERP registration.

---

### 5. Performance Monitoring

Track supplier performance using KPIs such as:

- On-Time Delivery (OTD)
- Product Quality
- Response Time
- Lead Time
- Cost Competitiveness

---

### 6. Continuous Improvement

Conduct regular business reviews and work together on process improvements.

---

## Best Practices

- Communicate regularly.
- Maintain accurate supplier records.
- Measure supplier performance consistently.
- Develop long-term strategic partnerships.
- Identify risks before they affect operations.

---

## Conclusion

Effective supplier management creates reliable supply chains, reduces costs, improves quality, and strengthens long-term business relationships.
