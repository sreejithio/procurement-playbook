# Procurement Process

## Overview

Procurement is the structured process of acquiring goods and services that meet business requirements while achieving the best value in terms of cost, quality, delivery, and supplier performance.

---

## Procurement Lifecycle

### 1. Identify Business Need

- Understand requirements
- Define specifications
- Estimate budget

---

### 2. Market Research

- Identify potential suppliers
- Compare available solutions
- Study pricing trends

---

### 3. Supplier Selection

- Issue RFQ or RFP
- Evaluate supplier proposals
- Assess quality, cost, and delivery

---

### 4. Negotiation

- Price negotiation
- Payment terms
- Delivery schedules
- Commercial agreements

---

### 5. Purchase Order

- Create Purchase Order (PO)
- Obtain approvals
- Send PO to supplier

---

### 6. Order Fulfilment

- Supplier manufactures or ships
- Track delivery
- Monitor lead time

---

### 7. Goods Receipt

- Inspect delivered items
- Verify quantity and quality
- Record receipt

---

### 8. Invoice & Payment

- Match PO, invoice, and goods receipt
- Approve invoice
- Process payment

---

### 9. Supplier Performance Review

- Delivery performance
- Product quality
- Responsiveness
- Cost competitiveness

---

## Key Objective

The goal of procurement is to obtain the right product, from the right supplier, at the right price, in the right quantity, delivered at the right time while minimizing risk and maximizing business value.
